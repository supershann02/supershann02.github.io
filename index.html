What I Like

Comics, Graphic Novels, and Manga

Comic books and graphic novels are a unique form of literature.
The combination of pictures and dialogue work perfectly together
to form a story. Most involve the use of action, which is difficult
to describe with words. Luckily, the pictures add much more in
terms of emotion and imagery.

My first experience with comics was with DC and Marvel
superheores. The stories were one-offs, rather simple
tales with small consequences for the characters, but
still entertaining. I later discovered comics with overlapping
stories on par with classic pieces of literature. There is
masterful tension, character development, and plots. Maybe
I'm overanalyzing, but it's how I think about my passions.

In basic terms, manga is the Japanese form of comics, though
there are noticable differences. Manga creators are more
indepedent, as they are not forced to collabrate with others.
The stories themselves are similar to novels, with several
side plots and a main plot. Though I started reading manga
a couple of years after discovering comics, I think I have read
more manga, as there is a better variety in stories. Manga is
is not afraid to explores genres and explore mature themes. My
personal favorates are action-associated with some deep story-
telling like "Fullmetal Alchemist" and "Attack on Titan".

Movies and Television

My family raised me with their passion for film. At a very young
age I watched 90s animated Disney movies and observed action
films behind the shoulders of my older siblings. As my sister
expressed her interest in screenwriting, I went to the theater
more and more. I developed my passion for comics by going to
see Marvel movies in the theater with my father. My mother
sometimes rented Studio Ghibli movies during my younger days,
which influenced my love for all forms of anime. My family, and
eventually myself, appreciated the many genres and styles of
film: the high-budget action romps, the Oscar-worthy indie
and the movies so bad they're good. I cherish the rhetoric
and uniqueness of some films and the pure entertainment of
of others.

My love for movies has only slightly translated to my love for
television. Personally, tv shows are a more difficult emotional
investment. My opinions are probably prefer anime and animated
shows. They are faster-paced, plot-driven, and most often
better written. Or maybe I'm still holding onto my childhood
with these innocent shows, though their writing sometimes
matures during its progression. I am rather attached to typical
shows that managed to captivate my attention. Most are
comedies that occasionally contain dark themes and mature
topics. The jokes in them are strange, yet comprehensible
and not childish. Despite my initial comments, I still
enjoy television, as most provide me with wonderful
characters, a well-developed story, and a good laugh.

Music: Albums and Bands

One of my first memories was in 2005: I was sitting in the
car as my parents drove to a summer vacation spot as "Feel
Good Inc" by Gorillaz played on the radio. I think the nostaglia
of that song is why Gorillaz is my favorite all-time favorite
band. Most people form their taste in music based on what they
listened to when they were younger. For me, it was alternative
rock. My parents listened to the new wave from their high school
and appreciated the garage rock revial of the early 2000s. My
siblings were also influencial, as the indie songs that echoed
through their rooms are tunes that I still enjoy today. When
looking for new music, I still use the references of my family.
I do enjoy other genres of music like rap, pop, and electronic,
though I usually prefer the alternative forms.

Video Games

The final category of my personality and interests is one that
I had no influences from my family. Sure, I began playing Wii
games with my siblings, my passion grew beyond theirs. My first
showing of my love for gaming was with my DS, where I played 2D
Sonic and movie tie-in games. I later got a 3DS and enjoyed higher
quality games. The biggest influence came in 2016 when I got a PS4
for my birthday. It came prepackaged with "Uncharted 4", which
soon became my favorite game of all-time. I was amazed by the
realistic graphics and emotional story perfectly mixed with the
action. I realized that video games could possibly be the perfect
way for me to express the stories within my head with striking
visuals. While most teenagers struggle to choose a major for college
I knew my main career choice for years. Creating a respected video
game is difficult, but I know that I am capable to overcome the
challenge.
